Nicola Küng's Movie Reel

Filmmaking is a process. It takes many years to develop ones style. Looking back, it's easy to be ashamed of ones beginnings. I don't feel like this. That's why I proudly present my entire portfolio of films below. Reaching back all the way to my very first experiments with cameras. Enjoy the journey!

2019

Van Diego

More of a video-slideshow than a movie. Shot during a ten-day roadtrip down the Californian Cost from San Francicso to San Diego.

Legartis – Corporate Video

Professional shoot for a young Swiss startup. Involved everything from drawing a storyboard, conducting and editing interviews to flying a drone.

2018

Autonomous Christmas Lab

Professional shoot for my lab at uni. Only had one week for the whole job and have never been as stressed out as when all robot operators had to be performing for the same shot.

Ascento

Roll-Out video for a robotics project I worked on myself. Marked my first time shooting interviews with two cameras and working extensively with text. With around four days of production time propably my most stressful job ever.

2016

Wertschöpfung

This video summarizes the journey of my team throughout a group project for our studies. Shot entirely on my phone but still suprisingly good quality.

RED

What to do on a boring afternoon with your musically-talented friend over? Maybe fool around a bit with an old keyboard and stop-motion technique?

2015

How To Make Cider

I have this cool uncle living in New York and like every cool uncle, he makes cider by himself. And plenty of other cool stuff too. This is my little hommage to his many skills.

Singapore

I made it a habit of mine to turn travels into little movies. This one came from a short trip to Singapore with my dad.

About Me

When I'm not making movies, I spend my time studying Mechanical Engineering in Zurich, Switzerland. Or the other way around. Making movies has been a passion of mine long before taking up my studies though. It all started when my god-mother gifted me a video camera for Christmas when I was twelve. Since then, I have been observing the world through the lens. Fascinated more by the tape of events rather than the events themselves. Because filmmaking has the magical ability to shape every aspect of how we perceive something. And that's what I find so dope about it.
